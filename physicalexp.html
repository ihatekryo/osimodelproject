Physical Layer

Coax -

Coax or a coaxial cable, a type of network cable used to transmit high-frequency signals like internet and video. A type of insulated cable that consists of a copper wire in the middle.

Fiber -

Fiber cable is a cable made of thin strands of glass or plastic that transmits data in the form of light pulses, enabling high-speed and high-bandwidth communication. Fiber is faster than coaxial cables.

Wireless -

In cybersecurity, wireless refers to any method of transmitting data without physical cables, usually using radio waves, Wi-Fi, or Bluetooth. Wireless connections are more vulnerable than physical cables.

Hub -

A hub is a basic device that connects multiple computers in a network and broadcasts data to all devices connected to it.

Repeaters -

A network repeater is a hardware device that receives a signal, regenerates its strength, and retransmits it to overcome distance limitations in wired and wireless networks.
